# Discount & Profitability Analysis Dashboard

## 1. Business Problem

Retail businesses frequently use discounts to drive sales growth. However, excessive discounting can reduce profitability and create loss-making product segments.

Management teams require visibility into:

* Discount impact on profit
* Loss-making products
* Profitability trends
* Pricing optimization opportunities

---

# 2. Project Objective

The objective of this project was to analyze the relationship between discounts, sales, and profitability to support pricing and promotional decision-making.

---

# 3. Dataset Overview

The project used retail transactional data containing:

* Sales
* Profit
* Discount percentages
* Product categories
* Sub-categories
* Customer segments
* Regions

# 6. KPI Definitions

### Total Discount

Total discount provided across all orders.

### Profit Loss Products

Products generating negative profit.

### Average Discount

Average discount percentage across transactions.

### Profitability Ratio

Profit relative to discounted sales.

# 8. Key Insights

### Higher discount levels frequently reduced profitability.

### Certain product segments consistently generated losses.

### Aggressive discounting did not always increase profit.

### Some categories maintained healthy margins despite discounts.

### Profitability risk increased significantly beyond specific discount thresholds.

---

# 9. Root Cause Analysis

### Excessive discount dependency reduced profit margins.

### Unoptimized pricing strategy impacted operational profitability.

### Low-margin products became unprofitable under heavy discounts.

### Regional pricing behavior created uneven profit performance.

---

# 10. Business Recommendations

* Limit high-risk discount levels
* Reevaluate loss-making products
* Improve pricing governance
* Introduce category-specific discount policies
* Monitor discount profitability continuously
